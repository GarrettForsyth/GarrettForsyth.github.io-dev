About Me

I am an aspiring software developer located in the Waterloo region, ON. I graduated from the University of Waterloo with a degree in honours physics, specializing in astrophysics.

I've always been impressed by the power of software to solve seemingly impossible problems. Its power and opportunity captivated me, so I decided to set out to master it myself. What I found was an entire new world.

My training in physics left me with skills in research and technical sophistication, which I harnessed to teach myself software development. I spend much of my free time reading about software, following online courses through resources such as Coursera, The Odin Project and Saylor Academy, and then reinforcing these concepts with assignments and projects (many of which can be found my github).

One of the most attractive things about software development, is the power to make your creations easily accessible to a large amount of people. With the right idea and execution, you can really make an impact in people's lives. I find great meaning in that, and it's my goal to put myself in a position where my work and ideas can help people.
